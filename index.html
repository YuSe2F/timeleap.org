Plot




Amidst the chaos of an unprecedented storm in the desert of Tunisia, a celestial object unlike any other slammed into our hero's backyard.

Curiosity overcoming fear, He ventured out under the dying storm to find the crash site.

But reaching for the object, a blinding light absorbed him, and in an instant he wasn't in his familiar desert anymore.

Towering structures, strange clothing, and a language he didn't understand – this was a Tunisia he'd never seen before.

Teleported back in time by the unearthly object, He must now navigate a past as foreign and unforgiving as the storm he left behind, all while searching for a way back to his own time.

Will he unravel the mysteries of the object and his sudden time travel, or will he be forever lost in this forgotten era of Tunisia?

Art style



Let's explore the potential of pixel art for our game TIMELEAP!
Here's why it could be a fantastic choice:

Charm and Nostalgia: Pixel art evokes a sense of charm and nostalgia, reminding players of classic titles and fostering a warm, inviting atmosphere.

Clarity and Readability: Simple visuals with clear outlines make it easier for players to identify objects and characters, especially important for fast-paced or top-down games.

Performance Advantages: Pixel art's smaller file sizes allow for smoother gameplay, especially beneficial for mobile platforms or resource-conscious projects.

Artistic Freedom: Despite its limitations, pixel art allows for incredible creativity. With careful design, it can create stunning visuals bursting with personality.
